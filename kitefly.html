KITE FLY

Another popular pastime in both Terengganu and Kelantan is kite flying, which the two states share. Each of these kites has the potential to reach a diameter of one metre and can grow to be as tall as the person who is flying it. There are many different forms available, and some of them are called Wau Bulan, Wau Kucing, and Wau Merak, to mention a few. They are cherished as a piece of art in their own right because they are crafted from thin rice paper and placed inside a bamboo frame that has been lightly carved. They are exquisitely embellished with tiny designs. The kites create humming sounds while they are airborne, and each one is pitted so that it can intersect the course of another kite in order to bring it down. An extremely thin layer of a type of glass powder that is capable of cutting the string of another kite is applied to the string of a kite. Those kites that fly the longest are the winners.
