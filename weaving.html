WEAVING CRAFT

For ages, weaving has been an integral aspect of life in this area. Rural residents have traditionally supplemented their income by weaving, which has long been practiced as a cottage industry.

There are a few different kinds of weaving: rattan, nipah leaf frond weaving, and pandanus (screw pine leaves). A variety of souvenir products, including attractive mats, coin purses, colorful slippers, pencil cases, handbags, gift bags, and hand-held fans are crafted from screw pine leaves.

Native peoples highly prize the Nipah tree's leaf fronds because of the many useful things they can fashion from them. These include traditional fruit baskets, woven lamp shades, traditional bird cages, and utensil baskets called "lekar.".
