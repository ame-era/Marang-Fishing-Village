TRADITIONAL FISHING METHODS

Bubu Trap

This ingenious trap is crafted from woven bamboo. It has a conical shape with long, slender leading poles that guide fish towards a central chamber. Fish enter the bubu seeking food or shelter and become trapped inside.

Pukat Tarik (Lift Net)

This communal fishing technique involves a large, rectangular net suspended from a wooden frame. The net is lowered into the water and then raised together by a group of fishermen working in unison. The coordinated effort allows them to scoop up a large quantity of fish at once.

Menjala (Drag Net)

This technique utilizes a long net dragged along the seabed by a boat or a group of fishermen wading in shallow waters. The net traps fish as it's pulled through the water.

Gillnet (Jaring)

Gillnets are large, vertical nets made of fine mesh. They are deployed in the water column and allowed to drift with the current. Fish gills get caught in the mesh as they attempt to swim through, trapping them in the net. While effective, gillnets require careful management to minimize bycatch of unintended species.
