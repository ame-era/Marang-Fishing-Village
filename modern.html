MODERN DEVELOPMENTS

GPS and Fishfinders

Gone are the days of relying solely on landmarks and intuition. Global Positioning Systems (GPS) provide fishermen with precise navigation, allowing them to locate specific fishing grounds and safely return to port. Fishfinders, using sonar technology, act as underwater eyes. They map the seabed, locate schools of fish, and even identify fish species based on their size and swimming patterns. This information empowers fishermen to target specific catches and maximize their efficiency.

Echo Sounders

Echo sounders are another valuable electronic tool. These devices emit sound waves that bounce off the seabed and any objects in the water column. By measuring the time it takes for the sound waves to return, echo sounders provide a detailed picture of the underwater environment. This allows fishermen to identify underwater structures that attract fish, such as reefs and wrecks, and adjust their fishing strategies accordingly.

Improved Boat Design

Traditional wooden boats, while beautiful and historic, have limitations. Modern fishing boats are constructed with fiberglass hulls, making them lighter, faster, and more durable. They are equipped with powerful engines that enable fishermen to travel further offshore, reach deeper waters, and explore new fishing grounds. Additionally, features like cabins provide shelter from the elements and storage space for longer fishing trips.

Refrigeration Units

Freshness is paramount when it comes to seafood. Modern fishing boats are equipped with refrigeration units that keep the catch chilled and prevent spoilage. This allows fishermen to extend their fishing trips and reach more distant markets, ultimately fetching higher prices for their catch.
